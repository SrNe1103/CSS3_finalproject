Cancer

Cancer is a genetic disease in wich some cells from the own body divide without control. Those cells eventually spread into other tissues, damaging the normal functions of the body.

Cancer can be described with the diferences between Cancer and Normal cells:

  • Growing in absence of signals
  • Ignores apoptotic (programmed cell death) signals
  • Capability to metastasize (spread to other tissues)
  • Hide from the immune system
  • Induce blood vessel growth nerby the tumor
(2025, NIH)

For instance, the Dendritic Cells travel through the body, collecting what is called antigens. Then, they present those antigens to the T cells.

T cells can identify if the antigen presented comes from a dangerous source.

Research Topics

The research topics that I'm interested in this field are the ones related to medicine. There are many diseases that can affect the immune system. Some of them are:

  • Autoimmune diseases
  • Immunodeficiency diseases
  • Allergies

Autoimmune diseases

Autoimmune diseases are those in which the immune system attacks the body's own cells. Some examples are:

  • Rheumatoid arthritis
  • Lupus
  • Celiac Disease

Immunodeficiency diseases

Immunodeficiency diseases are those in which the immune system is not able to fight infections. Some examples are:

  • Acquired Immunodeficiency Syndrome
  • Severe Combined Immunodeficiency

Allergies

Allergies are an overreaction of the immune system to a substance that is normally harmless. Some examples are allergies to:

  • Pollen
  • Dust mites
  • Cats and/or Dogs
  • Peanuts
